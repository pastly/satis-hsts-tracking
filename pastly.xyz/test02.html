Test for tracking via CSS resource

In the process of loading this page, an attempt was made to load CSS from a different domain. That domain redirected to another domain, which finally redirects to a resource on this domain. The important bit is that the two stops along the way try to set HSTS state in your browser for their domains. They both set a small max-age of 5 minutes because we aren't actually interested in tracking you.

In most browsers, the two domains along the way will be allowed to store their HSTS state in your browser. Safari won't; but Chrome will, Firfox will, and Tor Browser will until you close your browser.

The two domains are 08.hsts.satis.system33.pw and 09.hsts.satis.system33.pw.

You can check for their existence in your HSTS state in Chrome by visiting chrome://net-internals/#hsts and typing them in to the Query HSTS/PKP domain box.

You can do so in Firefox by closing your browser and greping for system33 in the file named SiteSecurityServiceState.txt in your profile directory.

You can do so in Safari by using a script to parse ~/Library/Cookies/HSTS.plist and greping for system33.

Implications

This can be used -- for example -- by an ad network or a large CDN to track a user across websites.
